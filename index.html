HumanX Lab at Dartmouth

The HumanX Lab for Cognitive Performance and Longevity is part of Dartmouth College’s Cluster for Improving Healthcare Outcomes through Sensory Technology (Academic Clusters). This Cluster unites Arts & Sciences, the Geisel School of Medicine, the Thayer School of Engineering, and the Tuck School of Business to design, validate, and translate sensory technologies that improve care. Its mission is to empower patients with personalized health insights and interventions and to enable caregivers from families to clinicians to provide better support.
The HumanX Lab was founded in 2024, when Professor Tam Vu joined Dartmouth as the Thomas A. and Georgina Tugwell Russo 1977 Distinguished Professor and was tasked to lead this Academic Cluster.
Dartmouth has a longstanding legacy in Artificial Intelligence as the birthplace of the term “AI” in 1956. HumanX builds on that history to establish Dartmouth leadership in AI-powered wearables, sensory systems, and digital health innovation, with a focus on longevity technologies and rural health.
HumanX Lab is an interdisciplinary R&D lab advancing wearable and AI systems to improve healthcare outcomes, extend longevity, and elevate human performance. From body and ambient sensors to edge-AI platforms, we invent and build the next-generation devices and AI platforms that integrate seamlessly into daily life and health systems, enabling scalable prevention and personalized therapy.

Our Vision

We design, build, and validate technologies that empower individuals to optimize cognitive performance, health, and longevity. By advancing adaptive computing, automated data collection, standardized cognitive metrics, personalized AI agents, non-pharmaceutical cognitive switches, and ultra-eﬃcient edge computing, we create solutions that are both scientifically rigorous and practically impactful. Our ultimate goal is to establish a future where people can understand, influence, and enhance their cognitive functions with accessible, trustworthy, and life-changing technologies.
